Model Performance
AUC-ROC Curve
Area Under the Receiver Operating Characteristic Curve (AUC ROC) measures the ability of the model to distinguish between positive and negative classes. A score of 1.00 indicates perfect classification.
Brier Score
Brier Score measures the accuracy of probabilistic predictions made by the model. Lower values indicate better calibration. In this case, a score of 0.0015 suggests high accuracy.
K-S Statistic
Kolmogorov-Smirnov (K-S) Statistic measures the maximum distance between the cumulative distribution functions of the predicted probabilities and actual outcomes. A value close to 1 indicates good model performance.
Lift Score
Lift Score measures the effectiveness of a classification model at identifying cases of interest. A high lift score, such as 98650.47, indicates strong predictive power.
